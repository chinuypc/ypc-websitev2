PROBLEM TO FIX

Accessibility testing tools are reporting that **several image alt attributes are too long**.

Some alt texts exceed recommended accessibility length and become difficult for:

• screen reader users
• accessibility parsers
• SEO crawlers

Alt text must be **concise, descriptive, and functional**, not keyword-stuffed or overly narrative.

---

ALT TEXT REQUIREMENTS

When rewriting alt text:

1. Keep alt text **short and precise**

Recommended length:
**5–15 words maximum**

Never exceed **125 characters** unless absolutely necessary.

---

2. Describe the **image content**, not marketing language.

Correct example:
“Members networking at a YP Club Dubai event”

Incorrect example:
“Exclusive high-energy networking experience bringing together ambitious professionals in Dubai through curated events”

---

3. Avoid:

• keyword stuffing
• full sentences when not necessary
• repeating surrounding captions
• marketing slogans

---

4. Preserve **SEO relevance when appropriate**

If the image is related to:

• Dubai networking
• entrepreneur events
• professional community
• business meetups

You may include those phrases **naturally** but never force them.

---

5. Decorative images

If an image is purely decorative and conveys no content:

Use:
alt=""

Do not fabricate descriptions for decorative graphics.
